Every organization gets a default site at `https://<siteName>.myclassroomio.com`. You can connect your own domain (for example `https://learn.yourcompany.com`) so students see your brand in the browser bar and in links.

## How ClassroomIO picks a base URL

When ClassroomIO builds a link for your organization, it resolves the host in this order:

1. **Verified custom domain** → `https://<your-domain>`
2. **Self-hosted override** (`DASHBOARD_ORIGIN` env var, if set)
3. **Development** → `http://localhost:5173`
4. **Academy URL** → `https://<siteName>.myclassroomio.com`
5. **Fallback** → `https://app.classroomio.com`

Most customer-facing course links use your org context and stop at step 1 or 4. The `app.classroomio.com` fallback applies when no academy URL is available.

## Set up your domain

<Steps>
<Step>
### Open domain settings

From the admin dashboard, go to **Settings → Organization**, then click **Edit domain** in the Custom Domain section.
</Step>

<Step>
### Enter your domain

Add the hostname you want (for example `learn.yourcompany.com`). Follow the DNS instructions ClassroomIO shows to verify ownership.
</Step>

<Step>
### Wait for verification

Verification can take time while DNS propagates. The domain must show as verified before links use it.
</Step>

<Step>
### Test on the live site

Open your custom domain in a browser. Sign in, open a course landing page, and confirm URLs stay on your domain.
</Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="warn" title="Verification required">
  An unverified custom domain is not used for generated links. Until verification succeeds, ClassroomIO keeps using `<siteName>.myclassroomio.com`.
</Callout>

## Which links use your org URL

| Link type | Uses org domain when verified? |
| --- | --- |
| Course enrollment (`/course/.../enroll`) | Yes |
| Course invite emails from **People** | Yes |
| Org site landing page and course catalog | Yes |
| Organization team invite (some flows) | May still use platform URL until fully aligned |

Course invites are documented in [How to Invite Students](/docs/invite-students). Those links should point at your academy, not `app.classroomio.com`, when your domain is verified.

## Email sender address

Invite and notification emails may still send from the ClassroomIO platform mail address. Your **organization name** appears in the message body; the sender inbox is not fully white-labeled today.

## Student login and redirects

Students should sign in on your academy so sessions and redirects stay on your academy URL. If login sends users to `app.classroomio.com` unexpectedly, check that the custom domain is verified and that you are sharing academy links (not the generic app URL).
