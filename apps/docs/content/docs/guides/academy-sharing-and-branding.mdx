title: Sharing Your Academy (Open Graph & Favicon)
description: How favicons, link previews, and social embeds work when you share your academy URL on Discord, Slack, iMessage, and other platforms.
---

When you share your academy URL — for example `https://ciodevs.myclassroomio.com` or your [custom domain](/guides/custom-domain) — chat apps and social networks build a **link preview** from two things:

1. **Meta tags** in the page HTML (title, description)
2. An **Open Graph image** (`og:image`) — the large preview thumbnail

ClassroomIO generates both automatically for cloud academies. This guide explains what you control in the dashboard and what to expect when you change branding.

For the public homepage editor, see [Customize Your Academy Landing Page](/guides/org-landing-page). For org name, logo, and theme, see [Customize Your Organization](/guides/customize-organization).

## Favicon (browser tab icon)

The favicon is the small icon shown in the browser tab when someone opens your public academy site.

### Set a custom favicon

<Steps>
<Step>
### Open Organization settings

Expand **Settings** in the sidebar, click **Organization**, then open the **Custom Domain** tab.

![Custom favicon settings](/docs/custom-favicon-setup.webp)
</Step>

<Step>
### Upload under Custom favicon

Use the image picker in the **Custom favicon** section and click **Save favicon**.
</Step>
</Steps>

### Fallback when no favicon is set

If you have **not** uploaded a custom favicon, ClassroomIO uses your **organization logo** (the same image as in **Settings → Organization**). You do not need to upload the same file twice unless you want a different crop or format for the tab icon.

:::info[Where the favicon appears]
The academy favicon is shown on **public org-site pages** (landing page, course catalog, public course pages). It is not applied to the admin dashboard, LMS, or login screens on `app.classroomio.com`.
:::

## Link previews (Open Graph)

When your academy homepage is shared, ClassroomIO provides:

| Meta field | Source |
| --- | --- |
| **Title** | Organization name + hero heading (from your landing page), e.g. `AI Academy \| Become a Certified AI Engineer` |
| **Description** | Hero subheading, or footer tagline if no subheading |
| **Preview image** | A generated **Academy** card with your logo, org name, theme colors, and (on paid plans) your tagline |

Free-plan academies include a small **Powered by ClassroomIO** watermark on the generated image. Paid plans show your tagline and no watermark.

### What the preview image looks like

The generated card includes:

- Your logo (top-left), or initials on a theme-colored background if you have no logo
- An **Academy** label and org name
- Theme-tinted background and accent colors from **Settings → Organization → brand color**
- Tagline text on paid plans (from landing page footer tagline or hero subheading)

## When you change name, theme, or tagline

| Change | Page title & description | Preview image |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Organization name** | Updates on next page load | Regenerated when the image is next requested |
| **Brand / theme color** | Unchanged | Regenerated — colors on the card update |
| **Hero subheading or footer tagline** | Description updates on next page load | Regenerated on paid plans (free plans do not show tagline on the image) |
| **Organization logo** | Unchanged | Regenerated |

:::warning[Previews can lag behind saves]
Text meta tags (title and description) update as soon as someone loads your site. The **thumbnail image** is cached for performance. After a branding change, visit your public academy URL once, then share the link again as a **new message** in Discord or Slack if the old preview still appears.
:::

### Refreshing Discord or Slack embeds

Chat apps cache link previews aggressively:

1. Save your changes in the dashboard.
2. Open your public academy URL in a browser (this helps regenerate the preview image).
3. Post the URL as a **new message** — editing an old message usually keeps the cached preview.

Discord in particular may cache failed previews for several minutes if the image was slow to generate on an earlier attempt.

## Course and lesson links

Public **course** pages use the course logo or banner for `og:image` when available. If a course has no banner, the preview may fall back to the course logo only. Org homepage links always use the dynamic Academy card described above.
