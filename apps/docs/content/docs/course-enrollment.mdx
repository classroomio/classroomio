Enrollment is how a student becomes a member of a course group and gets access to lessons, exercises, and the LMS. ClassroomIO supports several enrollment paths. Which ones are available depends on your course price, publish status, and settings.

## Enrollment paths at a glance

| Path | Who initiates it | Typical use |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Public self-enrollment** | Student | Open free courses on your landing page |
| **Email invite** | You (admin) | Enter email addresses; ClassroomIO sends each recipient a unique enrollment link |
| **Direct add** | You (admin) | Pick existing students from a list; they are enrolled instantly with no action needed from them |
| **Paid enrollment** | Student (after payment) or invite | Courses with a price set |

All paths end at the same place: the student is added to the course **group** and can open the course in the LMS.

## What students need before they can enroll

For **public** self-service paths (landing page, Explore), the course must be:

- **Published** — unpublished courses are not listed in Explore and do not accept open enrollment from the public site
- **Active** — deleted courses are blocked in all paths

**Email invites are an exception:** a valid invite token lets a student join a course even if it is unpublished. This lets you pre-enroll students before a course goes live. The course must still not be deleted.

## Public self-enrollment (free courses)

On a **free** course (`cost` is 0) with **Allow New Students** enabled, anyone who finds your course landing page can enroll:

1. They open the course landing page on your academy
2. They click **Enroll**
3. They sign in or sign up
4. They land on `/course/[slug]/enroll` and click **Join course**
5. They are enrolled and redirected to the LMS

No invite token is required. This is the default for free, published courses.

<Callout type="info" title="Allow New Students">
  In the course dashboard, go to **Settings** and find **Allow New Students**. When enabled, anyone can self-enroll from the course landing page. When disabled, the public **Enroll** button is hidden and open self-enrollment is blocked — but **course invite links** and **People → Add people** still work.
</Callout>

## Email invites

From **People → Add people**, you enter one or more email addresses and ClassroomIO sends each recipient a unique enrollment link. Each invite can have:

- An **expiry** (e.g. 24 hours, 7 days)
- A **max use** count (one-time or reusable)
- **Allowed emails** or domains (optional restriction)

When a student opens the link, they sign in (or sign up) and click **Join course**. ClassroomIO validates the token, expiry, and usage limit before enrolling them.

For step-by-step instructions, see [How to Invite Students](/docs/invite-students).

<Callout type="info" title="Email invites work on closed or unpublished courses">
  **Allow New Students** can be off and email invites still work. The toggle only blocks unsolicited self-enrollment from the landing page; it does not block people you explicitly invited. Invites also work on **unpublished** courses, so you can pre-enroll students before a course goes live.
</Callout>

## Direct add (People)

If a student already has an account, you can add them to the course directly — no email, no link, no action needed from them. They appear in the course immediately.

From the course dashboard, open **People → Add people**, select the students you want, and confirm. They are enrolled right away.

This does **not** require **Allow New Students** to be on. Use it for closed courses where you control exactly who gets access.

<Callout title="Already enrolled students">
  Revoking an invite or turning off public enrollment does not remove students who have already joined. It only blocks future enrollments through that path.
</Callout>

## Paid courses

When a course has a **price** set, open self-enrollment from the landing page is blocked unless the student pays or uses a valid invite (depending on your setup). Visitors who open the enrollment page without payment or a token see a message that they need to pay or be invited.

Paid courses are useful when you want to prevent casual sign-ups on a free landing page while still selling access or issuing invites to specific buyers.

## Free vs paid vs closed — quick reference

| Goal | Suggested setup |
| --- | --- |
| Anyone can join | Free course, published, **Allow New Students** on |
| Only people you email invites to | **Allow New Students** off (optional), send [email invites](/docs/invite-students) |
| Only people you add manually | **Allow New Students** off, use **People → Add people** to directly enroll existing students |
| Paywall + optional invites | Set a price, send invites to buyers who should skip or supplement checkout |
| Visible in catalog, enrollment closed | Published course, **Allow New Students** off, use invites or **People** to add members |
| All students in your org can join any published course | Enable **Internal Enrollment Only** in **Settings → Authentication → General**; students bypass per-course **Allow New Students** |

## Enrollment vs visibility

Restricting who can **join** a course is not the same as hiding it from every student view.

| Control | What it affects |
| --- | --- |
| **Published** | Whether the course is live on your academy and eligible for Explore |
| **Allow New Students** | Whether the public **Enroll** button works on the landing page |
| **Email invites** | Who can join via a link sent to their email (works even on unpublished or closed courses) |
| **People → Add people** | Who you add directly, with or without an invite link |
| **Price** | Whether open self-enrollment requires payment |
| **Internal Enrollment Only** (org-wide) | When on, any student in your organization can self-enroll in any published course, bypassing per-course **Allow New Students** |

<Callout type="warn" title="Explore shows published courses">
  **Explore** in the student LMS lists published courses the student has not enrolled in yet. Turning off **Allow New Students** blocks open enrollment from the landing page. It does **not** remove the course from Explore. ClassroomIO does not support hiding a published course from specific people in Explore today.
</Callout>

**Workarounds:**

- Keep the course **unpublished** until you are ready for students to discover it.
- Use **Allow New Students** off plus invites or **People** so only the people you choose can actually join.
- Accept that enrolled students may see the course in Explore even when they cannot self-enroll.

For where students see enrolled vs available courses, see [Student Dashboard](/docs/student-dashboard).

## People and membership

When a student enrolls or you add them from **People**, they become a member of the course **group** right away. They do not need to open a lesson first.

| State | What it means |
| --- | --- |
| **Active member** | Linked to a profile; appears in **People** with full details |
| **Pending** | Added by email before they sign in or finish onboarding |

The **People** tab in the course dashboard is the source of truth for course membership.

<Callout type="info" title="People vs Compliance status">
  **Compliance** courses also track completion status (for example **not started**). That is separate from membership. A student can appear in **People** as soon as they are enrolled while still showing **not started** on the Compliance dashboard until they begin the course.
</Callout>

## Organization-level access settings (separate from course enrollment)

Course enrollment is different from **organization signup**. In **Settings → Authentication → General**, you have two org-level controls that work together:

**Disable Signup / Allow Public Signups** — controls who can create an account on your academy. When signup is disabled (invite-only), only people you invite can register. This controls who can sign up, not which courses they can join.

**Internal Enrollment Only** — when enabled, any student in your organization can self-enroll in any published course without needing the course to have **Allow New Students** turned on. This is useful for internal academies: disable public signup, add employees via course invite links (which creates their account as a student in your organization), and they can then discover and join any other published course themselves without per-course admin work.

A student might be in your org but not enrolled in any course until you assign them, they self-enroll on a course with **Allow New Students** on, or **Internal Enrollment Only** is enabled org-wide.

## What students see after enrolling

Once enrolled, the course appears in the student LMS (home/dashboard). They can open lessons, submit exercises, and download materials according to course settings (for example, lesson download or certificate rules).

If a student follows an invite or enrollment link while logged out, ClassroomIO sends them through sign-in or sign-up first, then returns them to the enrollment page so they can finish joining.

## Common scenarios

<Steps>
<Step>
### Open cohort — anyone can join

Create a free course, publish it, and leave **Allow New Students** enabled. Share the landing page link.
</Step>

<Step>
### Private cohort — only invited emails

Turn **Allow New Students** off to hide public enrollment. From **People**, send email invites with expiry and optional one-time use. Only people with a valid invite link can join.
</Step>

<Step>
### Staff-only or hand-picked list

Turn **Allow New Students** off. Use **People → Add people** to directly enroll existing students, or send email invites to people who do not have an account yet. No one can self-enroll.
</Step>

<Step>
### Paid course

Set the course price on the landing page. Students pay to enroll, or you send invites to people who should receive complimentary access.
</Step>

<Step>
### Closed org academy — any student can join any course

Enable **Disable Signup** and **Internal Enrollment Only** in **Settings → Authentication → General**. Add employees by sending them course invite links from **People → Add people** — accepting a course invite creates their account as a student in your organization. Once they are students in your org, they can discover and self-enroll in any other published course from Explore without per-course setup. **Allow New Students** on individual courses is bypassed for students when this setting is on.
</Step>
</Steps>
